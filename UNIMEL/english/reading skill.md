### recognizing text structure and comprehension
- #### text structure
	text structure refer to the way author organizes language and information within the text to serve a specific purpose. Depending on the goal of the writer, text can be structured in a various ways to best comunicate information to a reader clearly and effectively
	- #### text structure type
		- Desciption
		- Sequence
		- Cause and effect
		- Compare and contrast
		- Problem and solution
- #### description
	Description is a text structure that is designed to create a vivid, detailed description of something
- #### Sequence
	There are two ways that sequence text structure can be used:
	- communicate a chronological sequence of events
	- steps in a procedure

	- #### sequence text for the chtonological purpose
		Sequence text structure for the purpose of chronological events is a common structure used in historical literature or non-fictional literature
	- #### sequence structure for procedural purpose
		Sequence structure for procedural information is common in educational literature from cooking instructions to scientific methods
		
- #### Cause and effect
	Cause and effect text structure is primarily used to communicate the causal relationship between an event, action, or idea and what follows

- #### Compare and contrast
	writers need to analyze the similarities and differences between two subjects. These comparisons are typically between people, places, events, ideas, or concepts
	
- #### Problem and solution
	the problem and solution text structure is designed to present a problem and transition into a proposed solution for said problem

- #### recognize and comprehend text structure
	 recognizing is to identify the way the author write. understanding how the text is orgainze make it easier for the reader to understand what message the author meanings depend on context of what they written
	- #### clue to recognizing and comprehend text structure
		- text signal
			text signal are clues to identifying text structure. text signal are word and phrases that indicate what  kind of text structure a reader will encounter as they read
			- #### text structure found in different text type
				- narative text
					most young reader are familiar with the language of *stories* (sometime called stories grammar) which usually include the following component
					- Expost ition: introduces the settings, character and conflic the story
					- Rising: introduce the challenge and obstacles that the character face
					- Climax: the turning point of the story, where the conflict is resolve
					- Falling action: describe the aftermath of the climax, as the character deal with the consequences of their actions
					- Resolution: ties up any loose ends and brings the story to a close
			- #### other example
				- Desciptive text
					- **Descriptive text** mixes different text structures in creating a vivid, multi-sensory picture in the reader’s mind
				- #### Expository text
					- **Expository text** doesn’t follow a typical sequence of events unfolding over time. Expository texts can also include all five of the common text structures, requiring readers to identify which structure is being used and for what purpose
